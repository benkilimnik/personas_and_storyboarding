Persona Assignment

GET this.

The end of the covid19-quiet-period has breathed new life into dining halls at Brown University. Although dining is limited to pickup at the moment, students have a gained a new option: pre-ordered, customized meals from Andrews Dining Hall via the GET mobile app. In this project, I set out to examine students' experiences with the UI of the GET app. Based on three interviews, I have crafted two personas and a storyboard showing the process of ordering food from start to finish.

Interface

The GET app is currently the only way to order food at Andrews. It is a public interface downloadable from the app store and features information on remaining meal credits as well as the Andrew's menu.

A meal can be scheduled for a specific pickup time and when it is ready for pickup, the user receives a push notification.

Interview

Questions

  1. How often have you opened the GET app today? How about this week?

  2. What do you use the GET app for?

  3. How long did it take to place an order at Andrews the first time you used the app?

  4. Have you ever been confused or frustrated by the app?

  5. How far in advance did you order food using the GET app interface today?

  6. Are there any features you wish the GET app had?

Key Observations

I interviewed 3 on-campus students: Joe, Stella, and Melinda. (I have changed their names for privacy.)

Melinda said she likes to open app several times a day to check for menu changes and enjoys customizing her meals. During the interview, she took twice as long as Joe and Stella to place an order and navigated between menu items more often.

Joe and Stella say they order their meals around 15 minutes before pickup, while Melinda orders her meals more than 1 hour in advance. Joe wishes there was a faster way to reorder meals because he tends to order the same meal over and over again.

Joe and Stella dislike the need to login and authenticate each time the app is opened, which Joe claims takes longer than ordering a meal. Melinda did not mention the login page.

All three thought the layout was confusing the first time they used the app, featuring many "meaningless" buttons and tabs. For instance, the favorites button (which allows favoriting a dining hall) and the saved addresses button were viewed as misleading or unnecessary. On the "Explore" tab, the greyed out "Start Order" button was viewed as particularly confusing because it looks like it can't be clicked on.

Responses

How often have you opened the GET app today? How about this week?

Joe: Twice today, 7 times this week.
Stella: Once today, 11 times this week.
Melinda: Four times today, around 30 times this week.

What do you use the GET app for?

Joe, Stella, and Melinda: To order food from Andrews or refill my bear bucks account.

Have you ever been confused or frustrated by the app?

Joe: It forces you to login with phone authentication every time you open the app, and no matter how many times I check the "remember me" box, it never does. It also takes a long time to load sometimes.

Stella: If you go to the explore tab and tap on a dining hall, the "Start Order" button is greyed out. I was confused the first time because everywhere else in the app a greyed out button can't be pushed and means I forgot to select something.

Melinda: Sometimes the transitions are disorienting. If you tap on a menu item, then go back by tapping on the x, the screen moves from right to left. This is counterintuitive, because it seems like you're going forward not back.

How long did it take to place an order at Andrews the first time you used the app?

Joe: Longer than it should have

Stella: 10 minutes. There are many distracting buttons. The first time I tapped on all the bottom navigation tabs only to realize they all lead to the same place: Andrews takeout or Bearbucks refill.

Melinda: 5 minutes

How far in advance did you order food using the GET app interface today?

Joe: 10 minutes max. I set my pickup time to ASAP every time and am usually already on my way to Andrews.

Stella: Several hours. I usually order all my meals in the morning and set pickup times for lunch and dinner.

Melinda: 15 minutes

Are there any features you wish the GET app had?

Joe: I wish I could favorite my meals so I can reorder them quickly without having to reselect everything. The current "Favorites" button is misleading, because it only allows me to favorite a dining hall. Since we only have Andrews (Blue Room is for staff only) this feature is pointless.

Stella: I wish there was a tab showing both the Andrew's and Vdub's menu on several days. This would really help me plan out my meals in advance. Right now I am having to switch between the Dining hall website and the app, which is annoying.

Melinda: It's hard to see all the options you have. Vdub menu isn't listed, so have to cross check with the dining website.

Personas

Based on these interviews, I have created two personas.

Roger

Roger is a junior at Brown University living on-campus. For convenience, he has chosen to stay on meal plan and his favorite dining hall is Andrews. Roger can't get enough of Andrew's breakfast burritos, but he only wishes he could get them faster.

Roger is studying Computer Science-Economics and practically runs Brown's annual hackathon as well as the Entrepreneurship Program. He is currently recruiting for summer internships in software engineering and consulting, and is always rushing from one zoom meeting to the next. As a result, his meal times need to be fast and efficient.

Roger represents a busy college student who doesn't want to spend much time thinking about what to eat. Like most people his age, Roger interacts with various apps on his smartphone on a daily basis, and likes to order food as quickly as possible. He is frustrated by the inability to quickly reorder a favorite meal using the GET app, and by the need to login everytime the app is launched.

Molly

Molly is a sophomore living on-campus at Brown studying Biology. She is conscious about her diet and makes an effort to eat healthy. As a result, Molly likes to be well informed about her dining options and likes customizing her meals from Andrews with the GET app.

Molly enjoys planning out her day in advance and often orders her meals ahead of time. She likes variety and checks the menu frequently to see if any of the options have changed.

Molly is representative of Brown students who like to plan out their meals in advance and be conscientious about what they eat. She checks the app's menu often and wishes she could compare it to other dining halls within the app. She finds the layout somewhat disorienting, and was especially confused by the greyed out "Start Order" button the first time she used the app.

Storyboard for Molly

This storyboard pertains to Molly's persona because:

  1. It shows that she was very happy to receive food from Andrews exactly how she ordered it with the GET app

  2. It demonstrates that she plans out her meals hours in advance, using the GET app's scheduled pickup functionality.

  3. It exemplifies her desire to consider her dining options and spend time browsing through the menu as she customizes her meals

  4. It expresses that she was disoriented by the many buttons and tabs when first opening the GET app, and that she was particularly confused by the greyed out order button on the "Explore" tab
